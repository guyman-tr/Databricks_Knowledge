# Dealing_dbo.Dealing_Staking_WelcomeEmail_Temp

## 1. Overview

**Operational staging table** holding a transient set of customer global IDs (GCIDs) targeted for crypto staking welcome emails on any given run of the staking email SP. The table is fully truncated before each write, making it a **current-state snapshot** with no history. Its single column intentionally limits its use to an email-dispatch handoff — downstream email execution reads it immediately after population.

**Row grain**: One row per GCID selected as a new staking-eligible customer for the current run.

---

## 2. Business Context

`SP_Staking_WelcomeEmail` (Author: Sarah Benchitrit, 2024-05-22) runs only on **Sundays and Wednesdays** (`DayNumberOfWeek_Sun_Start IN (3, 7)`). It identifies customers who:

1. Reside in an **allowed country** (exclusion list applied for US, Tangany-regulated, and None-regulation accounts).
2. Opened their **first staking-eligible crypto position** in the last 7 days (new stakers only, not repeat holders).
3. Match against supported crypto instruments in `Dealing_Staking_Parameters`.

After TRUNCATE + INSERT into this table, the SP also inserts into `EXE_dbo.EXE_Staking_AirDrop_sent_email` to record emails sent. This table serves as the **transient payload** between the eligibility computation and the email-dispatch downstream table.

**Supported staking cryptos**: ETH, ADA, TRX, SOL, DOT, NEAR, ATOM, AVAX, SUI, POL.

**Key business rules**:

- **TRUNCATE before each run**: No accumulation — the table always reflects the most recent run only.
- **Run days**: Sunday and Wednesday only; SP is a no-op on other days.
- **Country and regulation exclusions**: US-regulated and Tangany-regulated customers are excluded at source.
