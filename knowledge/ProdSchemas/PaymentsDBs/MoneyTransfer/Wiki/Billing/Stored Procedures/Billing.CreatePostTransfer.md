## 1. Business Meaning

Billing.CreatePostTransfer is the entry point for post-transfer actions - follow-up operations triggered after a primary money transfer has been initiated. Each call creates a new row in Billing.PostTransferActions, linking it to a parent transfer via TransferID and associating it with a ReferenceID for subsequent lookups.

Post-transfer actions represent secondary operations such as downstream fund movements to specific funding types, provider notifications, or reconciliation steps. They are decoupled from the main transfer lifecycle, allowing them to progress (or fail) independently.

The procedure is called by the MoneyTransfer application service after the primary transfer reaches a state that triggers post-processing (typically after successful creation or status advancement).

## 2. Business Logic

### 2.1 Post-Transfer Action Creation

**What**: Creates a new action with caller-specified status and type, linked to a parent transfer.

**Columns/Parameters Involved**: `TransferID`, `ReferenceID`, `Payload`, `FundingTypeID`, `PostTransferStatusID`, `PostTransferActionTypeID`

**Rules**:
- All fields are caller-specified - no hardcoded defaults in the SP itself (though PostTransferActionTypeID defaults to 1 at the column level)
- CreateDate is auto-set by DEFAULT constraint (GETUTCDATE())
- PostTransferActionID is auto-generated by IDENTITY
- No validation that TransferID exists in Billing.Transfers (no FK constraint)
- PostTransferStatusID typically starts at 1 (initial/in-progress), application-managed values

### 8.2 Verify the created action
```sql
SELECT PostTransferActionID, TransferID, ReferenceID, FundingTypeID,
       PostTransferStatusID, PostTransferActionTypeID, CreateDate
FROM Billing.PostTransferActions WITH (NOLOCK)
WHERE ReferenceID = '7418D7FB-CBFD-4288-A1CD-7B0C033E910D'
```

### 8.3 Count actions per transfer
```sql
SELECT TransferID, COUNT(*) AS ActionCount
FROM Billing.PostTransferActions WITH (NOLOCK)
WHERE PostTransferActionID > (SELECT MAX(PostTransferActionID) - 100 FROM Billing.PostTransferActions WITH (NOLOCK))
GROUP BY TransferID
ORDER BY ActionCount DESC
```
