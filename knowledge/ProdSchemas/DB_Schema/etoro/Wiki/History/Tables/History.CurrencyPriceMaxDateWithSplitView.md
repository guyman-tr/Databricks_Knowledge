## 1. Business Meaning

History.CurrencyPriceMaxDateWithSplitView is a denormalized price cache containing one row per instrument with its most recent known price, split-adjusted. The name explains its contents:
- **MaxDate**: The most recent price (max date)
- **WithSplitView**: Split-adjusted (prices accounting for historical stock splits)

9,339 rows - one for each active instrument in the system. The table is on the [MAIN] filegroup (not [HISTORY]), consistent with its role as a live price lookup rather than a historical archive.

Split-adjusted prices ensure that when a stock has undergone splits, the stored bid/ask values reflect the current trading price corrected for those splits. AskSpreaded and BidSpreaded include the spread (markup) applied on top of the raw market price.

This table is likely populated by a periodic job or the History.CurrencyPriceQueue_Process stored procedure that processes incoming prices from History.CurrencyPriceQueue.

## 2. Business Logic

### 2.1 Price Cache with Split Adjustment

**What**: Maintains the last known price for each instrument, adjusted for splits and with bid/ask spread applied.

**Columns**:
| Column | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| AskSpreaded | Ask price with spread added (what customers buy at) |
| BidSpreaded | Bid price with spread subtracted (what customers sell at) |
| Bid | Raw market bid price (no spread) |
| Ask | Raw market ask price (no spread) |
| PriceRateID | ID of the specific price tick this row is based on |
| Occurred | When this price was generated |

### 2.2 Split Adjustment

Prices in this table have been multiplied by the split ratio for instruments that have undergone stock splits. This ensures that InstrumentID=3's bid of 50.63 reflects the current post-split price, not the pre-split price.
