## 1. Business Meaning

Dictionary.OrderType classifies every type of order the eToro trading engine can process. Orders are the instructions sent to the execution engine — they specify WHAT action to take (buy/sell/modify), HOW to execute it (market/limit/stop), and in WHAT units (amount or units).

This table covers the full spectrum from simple market orders (OpenTrade) to complex conditional orders (BuyStop, SellLimit) and position modifications (EditStopLoss, EditTakeProfit, EditOverWeekend). The evolution of order types reflects eToro's product growth — early IDs (0-3) cover basic buy/sell, later IDs (13-20) add units-based trading and multi-position operations.

OrderTypeID is stored in order records and used by the execution engine to route each order to the correct processing pipeline.

## 2. Business Logic

### 2.1 Order Type Categories

**What**: Orders group into functional categories: market execution, pending entry, close, modification, and multi-position.

**Columns/Parameters Involved**: `OrderTypeID`, `Name`

**Rules**:

| Category | IDs | Description |
|----------|-----|-------------|
| **Market Orders** | 1 (OpenTrade), 15 (PendingOrderByUnits), 0 (PendingOrderByAmount) | Immediate execution at current market price. By amount ($) or by units (shares/coins). |
| **Entry from Order** | 2 (OpenTradeFromOrder) | Position opened because a pending entry order was triggered by price reaching the target level. |
| **Entry Orders** | 4 (BuyStop), 5 (BuyLimit), 6 (SellStop), 7 (SellLimit), 13 (EntryOrderByAmount), 16 (EntryOrderByUnits) | Conditional orders — execute when price reaches a specified level. Stop = execute when price moves through level; Limit = execute when price reaches level. |
| **Close Orders** | 3 (CloseTrade), 8 (CloseTradeOrder), 14 (ExitOrder), 19 (OrderForCloseByUnits), 20 (OrderForCloseMultiple) | Instructions to close existing positions — single or multiple at once. |
| **Modification Orders** | 9 (EditStopLoss), 10 (EditTakeProfit), 11 (EditOverWeekend), 12 (EditOverWeekendWithStopLossRate) | Modify parameters of an existing position without closing it. |
| **Execution Orders** | 17 (OrderForExecutionByAmount), 18 (OrderForExecutionByUnits) | Internal execution engine orders — generated by the system during order processing. |

### 2.2 Amount vs Units

**What**: eToro supports two order sizing methods: by dollar amount or by units (shares/coins).

**Columns/Parameters Involved**: `OrderTypeID`

**Rules**:
- **By Amount**: IDs 0 (PendingOrderByAmount), 13 (EntryOrderByAmount), 17 (OrderForExecutionByAmount) — user specifies "$500 of Apple"
- **By Units**: IDs 15 (PendingOrderByUnits), 16 (EntryOrderByUnits), 18 (OrderForExecutionByUnits), 19 (OrderForCloseByUnits) — user specifies "3 shares of Apple"
- Amount-based is the legacy default; units-based was added for REAL (fractional share) trading

## 3. Data Overview

| OrderTypeID | Name | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 0 | PendingOrderByAmount | Market order sized by dollar amount — "invest $500 in Apple". The most common order type for CFD trading. |
| 1 | OpenTrade | Market buy/sell at current price. Immediate execution. The simplest and most frequent order type. |
| 4 | OpenEntryBuyStopOrder | Buy when price rises to a specified level (breakout strategy). Becomes a market buy when price hits the stop level. |
| 5 | OpenEntryBuyLimitOrder | Buy when price drops to a specified level (dip-buying). Executes at or below the limit price. |
| 9 | EditStopLoss | Modify the stop-loss level on an existing open position. Does not create a new position — adjusts risk management parameters. |
| 10 | EditTakeProfit | Modify the take-profit level on an existing open position. Adjusts the auto-close-for-profit target. |
| 15 | PendingOrderByUnits | Market order sized by units (shares/coins) — "buy 3 shares of Apple". Added for REAL asset trading. |
| 20 | OrderForCloseMultiple | Close multiple positions simultaneously. Used for "Close All" or "Close All in Instrument" operations. |

## 8. Sample Queries

### 8.1 List all order types
```sql
SELECT OrderTypeID, RTRIM(Name) AS Name
FROM [Dictionary].[OrderType] WITH (NOLOCK) ORDER BY OrderTypeID;
```

### 8.2 Count recent orders by type
```sql
SELECT  RTRIM(ot.Name) AS OrderType, COUNT(*) AS OrderCount
FROM    [Trade].[OrderForOpen] o WITH (NOLOCK)
JOIN    [Dictionary].[OrderType] ot WITH (NOLOCK) ON o.OrderTypeID = ot.OrderTypeID
WHERE   o.InsertDateTime > DATEADD(DAY, -7, GETDATE())
GROUP BY ot.Name ORDER BY OrderCount DESC;
```
