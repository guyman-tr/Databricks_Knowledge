## 1. Business Meaning

History.tblaff_Tier2Members is the system-versioned temporal history table for dbo.tblaff_Tier2Members. It captures every historical version of tier-2 sub-affiliate membership records. In the multi-tier affiliate program, a parent affiliate can recruit sub-affiliates who generate commissions that cascade up to the parent. Each row represents one sub-affiliate recruitment relationship at a specific point in time.

This table is important for commission auditing and partner relationship management. When tier-2 relationships change - for example, a sub-affiliate is reassigned to a different parent, a membership record is corrected, or a sub-affiliate's external identifier is updated - every prior version is preserved here. This enables the finance and compliance teams to reconstruct the exact tier-2 structure at any historical date, which is critical for verifying cascading commission calculations and resolving disputes between parent affiliates and their sub-affiliates.

Data flows in automatically via SQL Server's temporal mechanism whenever rows in the base table dbo.tblaff_Tier2Members are updated or deleted. With 294 historical rows, tier-2 membership changes are relatively infrequent, reflecting the stability of most sub-affiliate relationships once established.

## 2. Business Logic

### 2.1 Tier-2 Membership Versioning

**What**: Tracks changes to sub-affiliate recruitment relationships in the multi-tier affiliate program.

**Columns/Parameters Involved**: `AffiliateLevelID`, `AffiliateID`, `NewMemberID`, `SubAffiliateID`, `ValidFrom`, `ValidTo`

**Rules**:
- AffiliateLevelID is the primary identifier for the tier membership record
- AffiliateID is the parent affiliate who recruited the sub-affiliate
- NewMemberID is the sub-affiliate being recruited (references the affiliate system)
- SubAffiliateID is an external identifier for the sub-affiliate (up to 1024 chars), used for integration with external tracking systems
- All columns except AffiliateLevelID are nullable, allowing partial records during setup
- Commissions generated by the sub-affiliate (NewMemberID) cascade up to the parent (AffiliateID)
- Changes to these relationships directly affect commission calculations

The table contains 294 historical rows representing superseded versions of tier-2 membership records. The moderate volume reflects the relative stability of sub-affiliate relationships, with changes occurring mainly during initial setup, reassignments, or corrections.

## 8. Sample Queries

### 8.1 View full tier-2 membership history for a parent affiliate
```sql
SELECT AffiliateLevelID, AffiliateID, NewMemberID, SubAffiliateID, ValidFrom, ValidTo
FROM dbo.tblaff_Tier2Members FOR SYSTEM_TIME ALL WITH (NOLOCK)
WHERE AffiliateID = 12345
ORDER BY NewMemberID, ValidFrom
```

### 8.2 Check tier-2 structure at a specific date
```sql

FROM dbo.tblaff_Tier2Members FOR SYSTEM_TIME AS OF '2025-06-01' WITH (NOLOCK)
WHERE AffiliateID = 12345
ORDER BY NewMemberID
```
